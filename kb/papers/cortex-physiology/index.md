# Cortex Physiology

## Scope

Cortical physiology examines how specific circuits in the cerebral cortex implement sensory, motor, and cognitive computations. Rather than studying networks in the abstract, this domain analyses the firing patterns, receptive fields, and circuit motifs of identified cell types in specific regions during behaviour. Methods span in vivo electrophysiology, calcium imaging, and optogenetics.

## Relevance

Cortical circuits are the computational benchmarks against which neuromorphic systems are ultimately measured. Place and grid cells demonstrate that high-dimensional cognitive variables can be encoded in low-dimensional population codes: a direct reference for energy-efficient representational schemes in hardware. Visual cortex provided the biological motivation for convolutional neural networks, and its actual circuit mechanisms reveal precisely where that analogy holds and where it breaks.
